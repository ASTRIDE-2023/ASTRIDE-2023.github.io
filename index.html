AIMS & SCOPE

The global Internet of Things (IoT) market size is projected to reach 2.5 trillion US Dollars by 2029 with billions of smart devices connected to the Internet by then. The emerging IoT revolution promises to impact almost every aspect of modern society. In an IoT setting, sensors help create a fine-grained digital representation of the evolving physical world, which can be used to implement new functionalities and/or bring transformative improvements to existing systems. One commonality to all these new technologies and scenarios is their reliance on the indiscriminate collection of data. Such data contains information based on which user behavior can be revealed, and hence, jeopardizing their privacy. Furthermore, the issue of user privacy is concerning because IoT devices and systems have been shown to be plagued with security vulnerabilities, which could grant access to sensitive data to malicious entities.

The existing behavior of IoT devices and systems contradicts the increasing expectations of privacy with more awareness and new legislation to protect individuals' privacy emerging worldwide (such as Europe’s GPDR, California’s CCPA, Brazil’s LGPD, and India’s PDPB). The fine-grained monitoring enabled by IoT also raises significant concerns about the data collector being able to infer properties such as religious beliefs, gender, and personal habits of individuals (e.g., smoker/non-smoker), among others, which individuals may not be comfortable sharing. Central to IoT applications is the data management technology that can represent, store, transform, and query sensor data. Given the importance of IoT, the data management community has been focusing on the special requirements of IoT systems due to the volume and high speeds of IoT data). On the other hand, the privacy and security community has studied potential attacks and vulnerabilities in IoT applications and systems and explored their corresponding detection and defense strategies.

The success and adoption of IoT data management systems depend on integrating privacy, security, and trust protections into them to guard against privacy and security risks posed by sensor-based continuous monitoring of individuals and their activities. The goal of this workshop is thus to help bridging the gap between two research communities ---- Security and Privacy Researchers with IoT Data management Researchers --- both from industry and academia. Additionally, the workshop would be an opportunity to involve privacy legislators and developers of modern smart systems.

TOPICS OF INTEREST

Topics of interest include, but are not limited to:

  • Secure hardware for IoT data management
  • Model-based security systems engineering
  • Understanding dependencies among security, reliability, and safety in CPS/IoT
  • High assurance security architectures
  • Intrusion and anomaly detection
  • Secure and privacy-preserving sensor data outsourcing
  • Privacy-by-design of IoT data management systems
  • Differentially private computing
  • Privacy-preserving machine learning and federated learning and analytics
  • Privacy regulations (GDPR, CCPA, CalOPPA) and their impact on IoT systems
  • Privacy-Preserving Data Analysis in IoT Systems
  • Inference control and access control policies
  • Authentication
  • Blockchain-based systems
  • Identity and access management
  • Security, privacy, and utility metrics
  • Application/use-cases in healthcare, environment, transportation, energy, etc.

SUBMISSION GUIDELINES

Authors are invited to submit original research contributions in English of up to 6 pages (without including references) in the IEEE camera-ready format (templates are available at the ICDE 2023 submission guidelines page) to the submission site https://cmt3.research.microsoft.com/ASTRIDE2023. Authors of accepted papers will be encouraged to submit an extended paper of up to 8 pages for final publication. Author are also invited to submit short papers up to 4 pages. The page limit includes any appendix and additional material. All accepted papers will appear in the formal Proceedings of the Conference Workshops published by IEEE CS Press, and will be included in the IEEE digital library.
